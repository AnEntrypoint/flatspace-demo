About Tigers

Tigers are the largest living cats and among the most recognizable animals in the world.

Physical Characteristics

Tigers are massive carnivores with a distinctive orange coat and black stripes. They can weigh up to 300 kg (660 lbs) and reach lengths of 3.3 meters (11 feet). Each tiger has a unique stripe pattern, like a fingerprint.

Habitat

Tigers are found primarily in Asia, inhabiting dense forests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps. They prefer areas with abundant prey and water sources.
