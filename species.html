Tiger Subspecies

Six subspecies of tigers exist today, each adapted to their specific environment.

Bengal Tiger

The most common subspecies found in India and Bangladesh

Siberian Tiger

The largest subspecies, found in far eastern Russia and northern China

Sumatran Tiger

The smallest subspecies, critically endangered and found in Indonesia

Malayan Tiger

Found in Malaysia and southern Thailand

Indochinese Tiger

Found across Southeast Asia

South China Tiger

Critically endangered subspecies with fewer than 30 individuals in the wild
